Data Model Definition

The object we will be working with is the abstract representation of interstellar spaceships. We will define multiple types of spaceships and the relevant data fields that are used to properly describe them to the desired granularity.

First of all, there will be two broad types of ships: Civilian and Military. Civilian ships, like their real-world analogues, are focused on transport of organic and inorganic goods (legal or contraband). Military ships are used for law enforcement and, when required, combat.

  • Ship Types
    • Civilian
    • Military

Both civilian and military ships can be separated into sub-categories based on their characteristics and roles. The following are common sub-categories:

  • Civilian Ships
    • Fuel Tankers
    • Civilian Transport Vessels
    • Cargo Freighters
  • Military Ships
    • Fighters
    • Corvettes
    • Frigates
    • Destroyers
    • Cruisers
    • Carriers

Now that categories are defined, let's discuss the data fields used to describe these ships. The most significant fields are:

  • Name - string
  • Hull characteristics
    • Hull strength [absorption] - uint32
    • Shield capacity [flux] - int64
  • Cargo characteristics
    • Cargo hold capacity [containers] - uint32
    • Liquid fuel capacity [tonnes] - uint32
  • Crew quarters
    • Service Crew - uint16
    • Marines (if applicable) - uint16
  • Armament
    • Weaponry output [mass/sec] - uint32
    • Hardpoints [num] - uint16
  • Engine
    • Travel Speed [km/s] - int64
    • Thrust [N] - uint64
    • Hyperspeed [ly/ys] - uint32
