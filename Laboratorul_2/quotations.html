In the style of our previous pages, we shall quote a wikipedia page on a fundamental detail of art history that regards the introduction of haiku poetry to the western world.

Haiku (俳句, haiku) is a very short form of Japanese poetry typically characterized by three qualities:
  • the essence of haiku is "cutting" (kiru),
  • a kireji ("cutting word") juxtaposing two images or ideas,
  • and a seasonal reference (kigo).
Haiku poetry was introduced to the West in the late 19th century by the Japanese writer Masaoka Shiki.
